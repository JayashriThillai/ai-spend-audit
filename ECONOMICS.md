# Product Economics

## Business Model

The product is designed as a lightweight SaaS platform focused on AI subscription optimization.


## Potential Revenue Streams

### Freemium Model

Free users:
- basic audit reports
- limited monthly audits

Paid users:
- advanced optimization insights
- multi-team analytics
- historical spending tracking
- benchmark comparisons


### Team Plans

Organizations could pay for:
- company-wide audit dashboards
- subscription optimization reports
- AI spending analytics


## Estimated Costs

### Infrastructure Costs

Current stack:
- Vercel hosting
- Supabase database

Estimated early-stage monthly cost:

| Service | Estimated Cost |
|---|---|
| Vercel | Free / Low |
| Supabase | Free / Low |
| Domain | $10–15/year |


### Future Scaling Costs

As usage grows:
- database costs increase
- analytics costs increase
- AI inference costs may increase
- monitoring infrastructure becomes necessary


## Customer Acquisition

Expected early acquisition methods:
- developer communities
- student networks
- LinkedIn posts
- referral sharing through audit links


## Retention Strategy

Users may return because:
- AI subscriptions change frequently
- pricing plans evolve
- teams grow over time
- optimization opportunities reappear


## Long-Term Opportunities

Potential future expansion:
- enterprise procurement optimization
- automated AI expense tracking
- finance dashboard integrations
- AI productivity benchmarking


## Risks

Potential business risks:
- AI providers lowering prices
- platforms bundling subscriptions
- low willingness to pay
- competition from larger SaaS management tools


## Key Assumption

Core assumption:

Users are currently overspending on overlapping AI subscriptions and lack visibility into total yearly costs.
